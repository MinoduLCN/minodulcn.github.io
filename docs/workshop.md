## Workshop Materials

### Atelier pratique 07/23
This first workshop was conducted on July 27 and 28, 2023, as a hands-on, station-based learning experience at the University of Kara, introducing participants to topics such as electronics, programming, environmental sensing, and climate adaptation.  
The full activity booklet, guiding participants through the workshop stations and exercises, is available here: [Activity booklet](https://github.com/MinoduLCN/minodulcn.github.io/blob/main/assets/pdf/Cahier_d'activit%C3%A9_AtelierPratique.pdf)

### Weather sounds 03/24
The workshop was conducted on March 2024 with students from the University of Kara. This workshop explored how weather sounds are associate with colors, emotions, and visual representations. 
The worksheet in English is available here: [Activity sheet](./assets/pdf/EvenementsMeteoEN fin.pdf)

### Paper Prototyping with stickers 11/24
These workshops were conducted in November 2024 with the Kara Uniiversity students and they conducted the workshop in local language with three rural communities (Tchitchao, Soumdina Haut, and Lama Saoudé) in the Kara region. Participants used a sticker-based paper prototyping method to explore needs, priorities, and expectations regarding a local community network. The participants expressed their ideas through drawing and sticker-based activities, focusing on relevant topics, desired services, and the role of weather information in their everyday practices.  
The materials used in this workshop are available here:  
[Paper Prototyping booklet](https://github.com/MinoduLCN/minodulcn.github.io/blob/main/assets/pdf/PaperPrototypingTaches.pdf)  
[Sticker set](https://github.com/MinoduLCN/minodulcn.github.io/blob/main/assets/pdf/CollectedIconsSticker.pdf)


### Clickthrough Prototype Testing 05/25
This workshop focused on testing and refining a local, offline-first interface through a clickthrough prototype. Participants interacted with different navigation concepts, icons, and audio-based content to evaluate usability, clarity, and cultural relevance.

### Card Sorting Game 05/25
This activity was inspired by prior research on culture-aware categorisations of interface elements by [Rodil et al.](https://link.springer.com/chapter/10.1007/978-3-642-40483-2_30). Participants grouped and organised different content images, icons and pictogramms to express their own understanding and priorities and to create a categorisation for the Local Community Network (LCN).
[Card set](https://github.com/MinoduLCN/minodulcn.github.io/blob/main/assets/pdf/CardSortingCategories.pdf)

### System Usability Score 03/26
This workshop focused on evaluating the usability of the local network system using a context-adapted System Usability Scale (SUS). Participants interacted with the system and provided structured feedback on clarity, usability, and overall experience. 
The adapted SUS was translated and simplified to ensure accessibility and comprehension in the local context, using visual scale
